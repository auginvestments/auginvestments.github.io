The Federal Reserve and Jerome Powell Hit Another Home Run for Gold and Silver

June 10th, 2020 Jerome Powell and the Fed released important economic projections and their plans on dealing with the fallout of COVID 19. Powell has stated, “we are not even thinking about thinking of raising rates”. Interest rates are set to hold steady at 0-0.25% until at least 2022, meanwhile the Fed has also stated they will continue their purchases of bonds in order to ensure adequate liquidity.

Powell also went on to discuss the long-term unemployment predictions which can be seen below.

This projection notes a quick recovery of some of the lost employment, however, it also indicates a long road to reach the employment levels of pre COVID. This will require continuous and further stimulus from the Fed and lower interest rates and a low yield on long term government bonds. The Fed and Jerome Powell are aware of this and are willing to do whatever it takes to get the economy back to where it was. The question is, how long will it take? This is not a quick shift that can be fixed by printing unlimited amounts of money. With the constant worry about a second wave and a huge run up in all stocks, it is very tempting to keep putting money in to the most beaten up stocks. This may work in the short-term however we urge you to stick with what is proven to work in a stay at home economy and even a COVID free economy; technology, gold and silver.

Low interest rates and the promise to keep interest rates low for the foreseeable future, continuing support from the Fed and yield control on the 10 year yield will catapult the price of gold, silver and the technology names that can experience massive amounts of growth in the coming years due to the growing adoption of technology.

Lastly, the Fed released their preliminary economic projections for US GDP and these numbers are the lowest ever estimated by the Fed in its history. These numbers can be found below.

They do however predict an uptick in the economy for 2021 and 2022 but again, in order to achieve these numbers, massive amounts of stimulus and continued interest rates near 0% are required. These factors along with a higher unemployment rate will catapult gold and silver and the continued stimulus and switch to innovation will spur the growth of technology. I recommend continuing staying long gold and silver and long US growth stocks. One way to do this is to own shares of QQQ and even IWF. These funds focus on large cap growth stocks that have been proven to continue to grow in all types of economies and I expect the trend to continue over the next few years, albeit with some bumps along the way however the overall trend will be higher.
