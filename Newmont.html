Newmont Corporation

Newmont Corporation is the largest publicly traded gold company with eight world-class assets in top-tier jurisdictions. Newmont also boasts the largest reserve base with 95.7Moz in gold reserves and experiences a $400M FCF/annum per $100 increase in gold price. With gold trading above $1,900 these are very large margins and Newmont will be able to raise significant amounts of cash to pay down outstanding debt, return cash to shareholders through its leading dividend or by purchasing other companies in order to further grow its earning potential.

Over the next five years, Newmont is set to produce on average of 7.5M Gold Equivalent Ounces (GEOs) per year at an average All In Sustaining Cost (AISC) of roughly $925 per ounce. With gold trading above $1,900 Newmont will produce over $6B in EBIDTA. If gold is able to maintain this level and potentially push higher we expect Newmont's share price to properly reflect its massive earnings potential and we value Newmont at roughly $74 USD per share with a $1,900 gold price and a $2.90 copper price.

We also find Newmont's previuous acqusiiton of Goldcorp's assets extremely accretive especially since the gold price has increased by roughly $600 per ounce since the merger. Our long term outlook is very bullish for gold as federal stimulus and low interest rates will be required for years to come which will enable gold prices to either continue to increase or stabailize in these elevated levels. Therefore, we expect Newmont to outperform the borader market through its impressive production profile.

Newmont shares have recently dropped from their high of $72.22 and now trade at $64.11. We see this 11% drop as an entry point and if further pullbacks occur to $60 and $58 we believe these supports should be bought as well.
