S&P Crosses 3,000 and Dow Crosses 25,000

As mentioned in my previous post, markets have grinded higher. However, recently we have seen a rotation out of high growth stocks (predominantly technology) into value stocks (predominantly financials). The reopening of the economy and renewed beliefs for the fight against COVID 19 has peaked the interest of investors into stocks that have been hardest hit by this Black Swan event. We have also witnessed a decline in gold and silver, especially in the miners.

I expect the rotation to shift back towards technology and precious metals with the focus going back towards work from home and the stimulus and debt effects that will once again propel gold and silver to new higher levels. We have experienced great resiliency in the US markets aided by the federal reserve. The S&P has crossed above key levels of resistance (3,000 and its 200-day moving average). Investors have been very bullish in this respect and expect the S&P to grind towards 3,100. I am very weary of all this optimism and exuberance that investors currently feel. I would not be surprised to see the overall markets stall at these levels and experience a roughly 10% drop or more. In that time, I expect the stay at home stocks and the precious metals miners to outperform once again. My top picks continue to be Silvercorp and Alacer gold/SSR Mining which I had previously posted about. The recent drawback in their stocks can be taken as a great entry point for further upside. Another top pick I have lined up for later this week is Kirkland Lake Gold (KL). Stay tuned for an in-depth analysis.
